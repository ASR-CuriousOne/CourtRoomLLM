--- Judge’s Ruling ---
Judge: In Favor of the Defendant.

The court has carefully considered the evidence and legal arguments presented in this case, and it is evident that the promotions made under GO.M. No.14 and GO.M. No.22, although later found to be unconstitutional, were made in good faith and in accordance with the law as it was understood at the time. The doctrine of prospective overruling, as clarified by the Supreme Court in V. Jagannadha Rao and Ors. v. State of Andhra Pradesh and Ors. (2001), is applicable in this case, and it is just and equitable to save the promotions made before the pronouncement of the order in Jagannadha Rao's case.

The petitioners, having held their positions for a considerable duration and acted in good faith, would suffer significant hardship if reverted to their original cadre. Furthermore, the State Government's reluctance to create supernumerary positions to accommodate the petitioners adds to the equities in their favor. The court is persuaded that upholding the promotions is not only legally sound but also ethically justifiable, as it balances the need for legal compliance with the demands of justice and fairness. The defendant's promotion to the post of Assistant Labour Officer is therefore upheld.
